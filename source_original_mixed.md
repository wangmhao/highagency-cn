# What is high agency?

# 

# 什么是高能动性？

I believe high agency might be the most important idea of the 21st century. When I first learned of high agency, it felt like a secret that had been hidden from me.

我相信，“高能动性”`[1]`或许是21世纪最重要的思想。当我第一次了解到它时，感觉就像是**拿到了一本不传于世的人生通关秘籍**，而此前我一直被蒙在鼓里。

> **【译者注1：能动性】**
>
> **能动性 (Agency)**，是著名心理学家阿尔伯特·班杜拉 (Albert Bandura) 在其社会认知理论中提出的核心概念。它特指个体运用自身资源，有意识地影响自身机能与生活境遇的能力。它并非对环境的被动反应，而强调人是自身体验的主动构建者。根据班杜拉的理论，能动性主要包含四个核心特征：**意向性** (Intentionality)、**预见性** (Forethought)、**自我反应** (Self-reactiveness) 和**自我反思** (Self-reflectiveness)。

So, what is it?

那么，它到底是什么？

In a 1964 public inquiry into whether the film *The Lovers* should be banned for its adult content, Supreme Court judge Justice Potter Stewart was asked to define pornography. He replied with the ultimate non-definition:

“I know it when I see it”.

High agency is one of those “I know it when I see it” ideas. And once you see it – you can’t unsee it. Before I explain it, let’s see it.

1964年，在一场关于是否应因成人内容而禁映电影《情人们》`[2]`的公开听证会上，最高法院的波特·斯图尔特大法官被要求给色情作品下一个定义。他给出了一个堪称绝妙的“非定义”式回答：

“我一看便知。”

“高能动性”就属于那种“我一看便知”的理念。而且，一旦你见识过它，就再也无法视若无睹。在解释它之前，不妨让我们先亲眼见证一番。

> 【译者注2】《情人们》(The Lovers, 法语: Les Amants)是1958年由法国导演路易·马勒执导的电影。

## How To Spot High Agency People

## 

## 如何识别高能动性的人

1. Weird teenage hobbies - Teenage years are the hardest time to go against social pressures. If they can go against the crowd as a teenager, they can go against the crowd as an adult.

   **拥有与众不同的青少年爱好** 青少年时期是抵抗社会压力最艰难的阶段。如果一个人在十几岁时就能对抗潮流，那么成年后他们同样能做到。

2. Treadmill energy - If you meet with them when you're tired and defeated, you leave the room ready to run a marathon on a treadmill with max incline.

   **人形充电桩** 当你疲惫不堪、备受打击时去见他们，离开时却感觉自己已经准备好在最高坡度的跑步机上跑一场马拉松。

3. You can never guess their opinion - The boxer who writes poetry. The advertiser obsessed with the history of war. The beauty queen who reads Nietzsche. If their beliefs don't line up with their stereotypes, they've exercised agency.

   **不落俗套** 写诗的拳击手，痴迷于战争史的广告人，阅读尼采的选美冠军。如果他们的信念与人们对其的刻板印象不符，这说明他们已经运用了能动性。

4. Immigrant mentality - If they've moved from their hometown, that's a good sign. If they've moved from their home country, that's an even greater sign. It takes agency to spot you're in the wrong place, resourcefulness to operationalize a move and a growth mindset to start from zero in a new location.

   **开拓者心态** 如果他们曾离开家乡，这是一个好迹象。如果他们曾离开祖国，那更是极佳的证明。发现自己身处错误的地方需要能动性，筹划并执行搬迁需要足智多谋，而在一个新地方从零开始则需要成长型思维。

5. Sends you niche content - A low agency trap is to look at the social engagement of content before deeming its quality. High agency people just look at the content. They spot upcoming trends very early.

   **“宝藏内容”挖掘机**低能动性陷阱之一，是在判断内容质量前先看它的社交热度。而高能动性的人只看内容本身。他们能很早地发现未来的趋势。

6. Mean to your face, nice behind your back - The social incentives are to be nice to people's faces and gossip behind their backs. To do the opposite requires agency because they're swimming against the social tide.

   **当面直言，背后维护** 人们在社交中的普遍倾向是当面一套，背后一套。反其道而行之则需要能动性，因为他们正逆着社交潮流而动。

7. Quit something of prestige - The miserable management consultant who breaks free from their golden handcuffs to become a stand-up comedian has to overcome momentum, social shame and sunk cost fallacy. The high agency person lives many lives and isn’t afraid to reinvent themselves — regardless of the perceived social cost.

   **敢于放弃光鲜亮丽之物** 一个痛苦不堪的管理顾问，挣脱“金手铐”的束缚，转行去当一名单口喜剧演员，他必须克服惯性、社会眼光和沉没成本谬误。高能动性的人活出多样人生，并且不畏惧重塑自我——无论需要付出多大的社会代价。

8. They don’t trust. They verify - A low agency trap is to be hypnotized by groupthink. High agency people refuse to passively download the current thing without first verifying it for themselves: • “They say” —> *Who is they?* *•* "Science says” —> *What is the science? Can I see the primary sources?* *• ”Misinformation —> What is your* *theory of knowledge**? Can you show me the first principles?**

   **不轻信，去验证** 低能动性陷阱之一，是被群体思维所催眠。高能动性的人拒绝被动地全盘接受当前的主流说法，而是会先亲自验证一番：“他们说”——>“他们”是谁？、“科学表明”——>具体是哪项科学？我能看看原始文献吗？、“这是误传”——>你的认知论基础是什么？你能为我展示其第一性原理吗？

9. Self-taught learning machines - Whether it’s learning to play their favourite song on the Saxophone or deconstructing how 3D printers work — they start from zero and use agency to climb up the knowledge ladder. Tesla, Da Vinci and Darwin didn’t ask for permission from institutions to just do things.

   **自学成才的学习机器** 无论是学习用萨克斯管吹奏自己喜欢的歌曲，还是解构3D打印机的工作原理——他们都能从零开始，并运用能动性攀登知识的阶梯。特斯拉、达芬奇和达尔文可没有请求机构的许可能让他们去做事。

10. They question the question - Before rushing to answer your question, they question whether it’s the right question to answer. They know the right answer to the wrong question is worse than no answer to the right question.

    质疑问题本身 在匆忙回答你的问题之前，他们会先质疑这是否是那个真正该回答的问题。他们深知，为一个错误的问题找到正确答案，其后果比无法回答一个正确的问题更为严重。

Now you’ve seen it — let’s try to explain it...

现在你已经亲眼见证了——让我们来试着解释它……

## The High Agency Spectrum

## 

## 高能动性光谱

The person you'd call stuck in a 3rd world jail cell represents the most high agency person you know because it's the ultimate live problem -- on steroids.

那个在第三世界国家监狱里你会打电话求助的人，代表了你所认识的能动性最高的人，因为这是一个**加强版的终极实战难题**。

You’re giving the person you would call a seemingly impossible problem alone in the real world with no guidebook and still betting on them finding a solution.

你将一个看似无解的难题，抛给了那个要在现实世界里孤军奋战、且没有任何行动指南的人，但你依然把赌注押在了他身上，赌他能找到解决之道。

![img](./images/image-5.avif)

If you created a room with everyone you'd call when stuck in a 3rd world jail cell, what would that high agency room have in common?

It's not age, gender, race, education, job titles or politics. It’s not optimism or pessimism either.

Optimism states the glass is half full. Pessimism states the glass is half empty.

High agency states you’re a tap. You look in the mirror and see a giant tap staring back at you.

如果你把那些在“第三世界监狱困境”中你会打电话求助的人都聚集到一个房间里，这间“高能动性”屋子里的人们会有什么共同点？

答案无关年龄、性别、种族、教育背景、职位头衔或政治立场，甚至也无关乐观或悲观。

乐观主义者说，杯子是半满的。

悲观主义者说，杯子是半空的。

而高能动性则认为，你根本不该关心杯子——**你自己就是水龙头**。你看着镜子，看到一个巨大的水龙头正回望着你。

![img](./images/image-6.avif)

The one big thing everyone in that high agency room has in common: They are happening to life. They don’t view the future as a static entity. They view it as something to be shaped by human action.

那间“高能动性”屋子里的人们，他们最大的共同点是：他们并非被动地承受人生，而是主动地创造人生。 他们不把命运看作某种定数，而是坚信**它能由自己亲手塑造**。

*"When you're told that something is impossible, is that the end of the conversation, or does that start a second dialogue in your mind? How to get around whoever it is that's just told you that you can't do something"* - Eric Weinstein on high agency.

“当你被告知某件事不可能时，这会是谈话的终点，还是会在你脑中开启另一场内心对话——一场关于“**如何绕过那个刚刚说你做不到的人，依然把事搞定”**的对话？”

——埃里克·温斯坦 (Eric Weinstein) 论高能动性

High agency is such an important idea because the more agency an individual or society has -- the more problems they can solve.

“高能动性”之所以如此重要，是因为无论对个人还是社会而言，其拥有的能动性越强，能解决的难题就越多。

High agency can be a confusing idea to understand because it’s not just one idea. It’s a combination of three distinct skills rarely found together:

- Clear thinking
- Bias to action
- Disagreeability

High agency is like a tricycle. If you remove one of the wheels, it stops working.

“高能动性”这个概念可能难以理解，因为它并非单一理念，而是三种罕见技能的结合体：

- **思路清晰 (Clear thinking)**
- **行动本能 (Bias to action)**
- **不盲从的智慧 (Disagreeability)**

高能动性就像一辆三轮车。如果你拆掉任何一个轮子，它就无法前行了。

![img](./images/image-7.avif)

It's impossible to imagine someone breaking you out of a 3rd world jail cell without all three.

If they can't think clearly, they will charge ahead with the first bad plan that pops into their head.

If they lack a bias for action, they'll never move their ideas from theory into the real world.

If they aren't disagreeable, they'll quit and conform when someone in authority tells them "No".

你无法想象，一个不三者兼备的人，能把你从第三世界国家的监狱里弄出来。

- 如果他们**思路不清**，就会凭着一时冲动，去执行脑子里冒出的第一个馊主意。
- 如果他们缺乏**行动本能**，他们的想法将永远停留在理论层面，无法付诸现实。
- 如果他们缺乏**不盲从的智慧**，那么一旦有权威人士说“不”，他们就会轻易放弃、选择顺从。

# Part 1 - High Agency Software

# **高能动性的“心智软件”**

People on the high agency spectrum don't passively download their worldview from others.

However, many have independently installed five similar lines of software in their heads:

身处“高能动性光谱”顶端的人，不会从他人那里被动地“下载”自己的世界观。

然而，他们中的许多人，都不约而同地在自己的“心智软件”里**内置**了五条相似的核心指令：

1. ## There’s no unsolvable problem

世上没有解不开的难题

The human brain is a question-answering machine. If you ask it: “What’s good in my life?” — it will find answers. If you ask it: “What’s bad in my life?” — it will find answers.

When faced with a problem, ask your brain, “Does this defy the laws of physics?” — it will reply with “No” — and begin generating ludicrous ways this could be possible. Asking your brain this question wipes your mental whiteboard around the problem and knocks on the door of your sleeping creativity.

If your problem doesn’t defy the laws of physics, it’s not an unsolvable problem — regardless of what other people say.

Imagine you’re in a casino staring at a Roulette wheel.

And you’re presented with this problem: You must beat the house.

人脑就是一台“答案生成器”。如果你问它：“我的生活中有哪些好事？”——它就会找出答案。如果你问它：“我的生活中有哪些坏事？”——它同样会找出答案。

当面临一个难题时，问你的大脑：“这件事违背物理定律吗？”——它会回答“没有”——然后便会开始构想出各种**天马行空的方案**来让这件事成为可能。向你的大脑提出这个问题，能瞬间清空你脑中关于此事的思维白板，并叩开你沉睡创造力的大门。

只要你的难题不违背物理定律，它就不是一个无法解决的难题——无论旁人怎么说。

想象一下，你正身处一家赌场，凝视着一张轮盘赌桌。

你面临的难题是：**必须打败庄家**。

![img](./images/image-11.avif)

![img](./images/image-10.avif)

You could reply: “*Well that’s impossible. Roulette is a game of luck — not a game of skill*”

But does the problem defy the laws of physics? Do you need to defy the laws of thermodynamics? Or break Newton’s laws of motion?

In 1961, Claude Shannon and Ed Thorp decided hacking roulette didn’t defy the laws of physics — therefore it was a solvable problem. 

To hack roulette, they built the first-ever wearable computer. The device transmitted information about roulette wheels via a cigarette packet-sized box that they placed in their shoe. Shannon and Thorp would use a toe-operating switch to input data on the ball’s speed and wheel rotation to predict a landing zone. They improved their odds by 44% and outsmarted the house.

你可能会反驳：“不可能。轮盘赌是纯靠运气的游戏，而不是技巧游戏。”

但这个难题违背了物理定律吗？你需要去推翻热力学定律，或是打破牛顿运动定律吗？

1961年，克劳德·香农 (Claude Shannon) 和爱德华·索普 (Ed Thorp) 认定，**破解轮盘赌**并不违背物理定律——因此，这是一个可以解决的难题。

为了破解轮盘赌，他们制造了史上第一台可穿戴计算机。这个设备通过一个放在他们鞋里、香烟盒大小的盒子来传输轮盘的相关信息。香农和索普会用一个脚趾操控的开关来输入小球的速度和轮盘的转速，从而预测出落点区域。他们将胜算提高了44%，并最终**智取了庄家**。

![img](./images/image-12.avif)

If it doesn't defy the laws of physics - you can't give fixed odds on whether a human will solve the problem because of human agency.

David Deutsch uses the example of an asteroid about to hit Earth one year from now. If penguins were in charge of the response, you could calculate fixed odds based on the physics of the asteroid what will happen to the earth. With humans in charge, it’s impossible to calculate fixed odds.

If we choose the low agency route of passively accepting the Earth will be wiped out, the odds of annihilation increase. If we choose the high agency route of building laser beams that blow the asteroids out of the sky, the probability of annihilation decreases.

只要一件事不违背物理定律，你就无法预测人类解决它的确切概率——因为人类的能动性本身就是最大的变数。

物理学家戴维·多伊奇 (David Deutsch) 曾举过一个例子：假设一颗小行星将在一年后撞击地球。如果让企鹅来负责应对，你完全可以根据小行星的物理轨迹，计算出地球命运的固定概率。但如果由人类来负责，计算出固定的概率就变得不可能了。

如果我们选择低能动性的路线，被动接受毁灭的命运，那么地球毁灭的概率就会增加。如果我们选择高能动性的路线，去建造能将小行星在太空中击毁的激光束，那么地球毁灭的概率就会降低。

1. ## There’s no way

法无定法

There was a special moment in tennis history: The three greatest of all time -- competed at the exact same time. Nadal. Djokovic. Federer.

Matthew Syed tells a wonderful story of watching them warm up before Wimbledon.

网球史上曾有一个特殊时刻：三位史上最伟大的选手——纳达尔、德约科维奇、费德勒——在同一个时代同场竞技。

作家马修·萨伊德 (Matthew Syed) 讲述过一个精彩的故事，关于他在温布尔登网球公开赛前观看这三人热身的经历。

![img](./images/image-14.avif)

When Nadal warmed up, it was sheer aggression. He sprinted up and down like a man possessed with his shirt dripping in sweat.

When Djokovic warmed up, it was pure emotionless calibration. He was measured and scientific with every shot.

When Federer warmed up, you could hear him giggling before he arrived. He was doing trick shots, caressing the ball and exploring his own creativity.

There was no 'way' of doing things they all repeated -- except doing what worked for them. 

纳达尔热身时，充满了纯粹的攻击性。他如同着了魔一般来回冲刺，汗水湿透了球衣。

1. ## There’s no normal

3. If you’re 100% honest with your feedback on people’s business ideas, the short-term reaction is anger. In the long term, you become one of the few people they trust.

陷入这种陷阱的人，常常花费无数小时去思考——却没有一刻是真正想清楚了。普通人每天会产生一万到六万个念头。你还记得昨天具体的任何一个念头吗？

1. ## The Midwit Trap“

中智”陷阱

![img](./images/image-49.jpeg)

The midwit trap overcomplicates agency. It comes from the midwit meme: The dreaded midwit in the middle.

中智陷阱，在于将能动性过度复杂化。它源自那张著名的“Midwit陷阱”梗图：那个位于钟形曲线中间、令人头疼的家伙。

![img](./images/image-51.avif)

The guy on the left isn’t smart enough to overcomplicate the simple actions. The guy on the right is smart enough to reduce complexity and focus on the simple actions. The midwit in the middle lives in the danger zone and mistakes simplicity for stupidity.

The less intelligent guy on the left often has more agency than the midwit because he didn’t have the cognitive horsepower to bullshit himself.

左边那个头脑简单的人，还没聪明到能把简单的行动复杂化。右边那个真正聪明的人，则有足够的智慧去**化繁为简**，专注于简单的行动。而中间那个“中智”，则活在危险地带，常常把简单当成愚蠢。

所以，左边那个不那么聪明的人，其能动性往往比中间的“中智”更高——因为他根本没那个**脑力**去用一套花里胡哨的理论**把自己给绕进去**。

**Midwit Trap Escape Route:** ***What would the guy on the left do? Find it via inversion.***

Step 1 - Stop trying to be the guy on the right - The midwit mistake is to try and be the smart guy on the right. 

Step 2 - Become the guy on the left - Find the simple ideas via inversion. Flip the problem around. And take them seriously before adding any complexity.

E.g. "I want to become a better writer”

The midwit will start learning about writers' coffee-drinking routines or researching the perfect keyboard to enhance their word count. Instead, how would you ensure you become a worse writer?

1. Do not write
2. Write inconsistently
3. Write about things you find boring

Flip them around and you've found the simple ideas the guy on the left will come up with:

1. Write
2. Write consistently
3. Write about things that excite you

**“中智”陷阱的逃脱方案：用逆向思考，找到左边那家伙的简单做法**

**第一步：放弃成为右边那个天才。** “中智”最常犯的错误，就是总想着一步登天，成为右边那个聪明的家伙。

**第二步：先成为左边那个家伙。** 通过**逆向思考**找到那些简单的答案。把问题整个反过来想。并且，在增加任何复杂性之前，都必须先严肃地对待这些简单的答案。

- 根本不写。
- 三天打鱼，两天晒网。
- 专写那些自己都觉得无聊的东西。

现在，把这些答案完全颠倒过来，你就找到了左边那个家伙会想到的那些简单法则：

- **去写。**
- **持续地写。**
- **写那些能让你兴奋的东西。**

1. ## The Attachment Trap“

The attachment trap is too attached to past assumptions to see new high agency options. You become the man with a hammer who can only see a nail.

The three most powerful mind-altering drugs: DMT, meth, and momentum.

The root cause of the attachment prison is last principles thinking -- the opposite of first principles thinking. This is where your mind has assumptions about reality, treats these assumptions as unquestionable facts — and then starts looking for evidence to support it without once questioning the assumptions.

“执念”陷阱，在于你被过往的假设束缚得太紧，以至于看不到新的、更高能动性的选项。你就变成了那个**手里拿着锤子、看什么都像钉子**的人。

世上最烈性的三种精神药物是：DMT（二甲基色胺）、冰毒，以及**惯性**。

这种“执念”牢笼的根源，在于“定论式思维”——它与“第一性原理思维”完全相反。在这种思维模式下，你的大脑会先对现实做出一系列假设，然后将这些假设当作不容置疑的事实，并开始寻找证据来支持它，却从未对假设本身提出过一次质疑。

![img](./images/image-53.avif)

**Attachment Trap Escape Route:** ***What would I do if I had 10x the agency?*** **“执念”陷阱的逃脱方案：问自己，“如果我的能动性是现在的十倍，我会怎么做？”**

![img](./images/image-54.avif)

This question from Nick throws a first principles grenade into your mind. It forces you to start the problem again from a fresh perspective free of past assumptions. You often realize the real problem wasn’t the problem itself. It was the assumptions you were viewing it through.

尼克提出的这个问题，就像一枚**第一性原理的手雷**，被扔进了你的脑海。它迫使你挣脱过往假设的束缚，从一个全新的视角重新审视问题。你常常会因此顿悟，**真正的问题往往并非问题本身，而是你审视它时所依赖的那些潜在假设**。

Rumination can be reframed as a smoke alarm for action. If you catch yourself ruminating, reply with *How can I take action on this?*

In the 5 years spent ruminating on the theoretical decision to move from Doncaster to New York, you could’ve collected practical living data on New York, Los Angeles, Rio De Janeiro, Tokyo, Sydney, London and Reykjavík. 

你可以把“精神内耗”重新定义为**行动的警报器**。一旦你发现自己陷入内耗，就立刻用这个问题来回应：“我现在就能为此做点什么？”

## 5. The Overwhelm Trap“

## 望而生畏”陷阱

![img](./images/image-57.avif)

The overwhelm trap paralyses agency. The task is so daunting you don’t know where to begin. You drown in the sheer potential number of actions ahead of you.

1. ## Crack Addict Urgency

瘾君子般的行动力

Thought experiment: If the reward for solving the problem was a trillion dollars, and the consequence for not solving the problem was the death penalty -- would you find a way to solve the problem?

There's few great men of history with more bias to action than a crack cocaine addict sourcing their next hit. Well meaning intentions unfortunately do not create action. E.g. New Year's resolutions, the Mecca of well meaning intentions, have a reported ∼91% failure rate. If you contrast this with publicly announcing you’re going to run a marathon for charity, the failure rates reverse.

The person announcing publicly they're going to run a marathon for charity puts the gun behind their head. They’ve burnt the boats.

思想实验：如果解决一个难题的奖励是一万亿美元，而失败的后果是死刑——你会想办法解决它吗？

历史上，很少有伟人的行动本能，能比一个瘾君子寻找下一剂毒品时更强。不幸的是，美好的意愿并不能催生行动。例如，新年愿望——这个美好意愿的“麦加圣地”——据报道有约91%的失败率。但如果你将此与“公开宣布要为慈善跑一场马拉松”相比，失败率则会逆转。

公开宣布为慈善跑马拉松的人，无异于把枪顶在了自己的脑后。他们已经**破釜沉舟**。

Here's some iconic incentive schemes to burn the boats and embrace the crack addict mindset:

A. The Ticking Clock

Elon Musk proposed to George Hotz the following to build a vision system for Tesla autopilot：

$12 million payment if it was delivered the next day. 

For every month that passes where it's not delivered, the payment would be reduced by $1 million.

If you had an incentive scheme where dollars disappear like a ticking clock each minute, would you need motivation to take action? 

B. The Tattoo

Mr. Beast and his friend were regularly skipping workouts, so they created a bet:

If one of them skipped a workout , they had to get the tattoo of the other person.

Guess how many workouts they skipped that year? Zero.

Discipline is a candle flame to action. Incentives and consequences are a nuclear reactor to action.

Have agency over your action and design incentives and consequences for not taking action: Set a public deadline. Invite people to the diary event. Bet on yourself.

以下是一些经典的激励机制，能帮你破釜沉舟，拥抱那种瘾君子般的心态：

**A.

滴答作响的时钟**

埃隆·马斯克 (Elon Musk) 曾向乔治·霍兹 (George Hotz) 提出如下方案，来为特斯拉的自动驾驶系统构建视觉系统：

如果第二天就能交付，报酬是1200万美元。此后每延迟一个月交付，报酬就减少100万美元。

试想一下，如果你的激励机制是，奖金每分钟都在像倒计时的时钟一样消失，你还需要所谓的“动力”才去行动吗？

**B.

纹身赌注**

“油管博主”野兽先生 (Mr. Beast) 和他的朋友过去经常逃避锻炼，于是他们设下了一个赌局：

如果谁敢逃避一次锻炼——就必须在身上纹上对方的肖像。

猜猜看，那一年他们逃了多少次锻炼？零次。

**自律之于行动，不过是摇曳的烛火；而激励与后果，则是驱动行动的核反应堆。**

主动掌控你的行动，为你可能出现的不作为，设计好相应的激励与后果：设定一个公开的截止日期，邀请别人加入你的日程事件，把赌注押在自己身上。
